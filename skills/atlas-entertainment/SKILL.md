# ATLAS Skill — Entertainment & Programming

Definitive reference for selecting, booking, managing, and programming all entertainment elements at events — from live music and keynote speakers to interactive experiences and children's entertainment. Covers the full lifecycle from concept and booking through contracts, rehearsal, and show management, with deep knowledge of Portuguese licensing, cultural entertainment, and market pricing.

## When to activate

Invoke `/atlas-entertainment` (or trigger automatically) when:
- User needs to plan entertainment for any event
- User asks about live music, DJ, performers, speakers, or activities
- User needs to design the event programme/flow (energy curve)
- User asks about artist booking, contracts, or rider requirements
- User needs MC/host selection or script development
- User asks about music licensing (SPA) in Portugal
- User mentions "entretenimento", "animacao", "musica", "programa", "orador"
- After `atlas-briefing` defines the event concept and before `atlas-av` specs the technical requirements

Do NOT use when:
- User needs only AV/technical production (use `atlas-av`)
- User needs only stage/scenic design (use `atlas-staging`)
- User needs photography/videography (use `atlas-photo-video`)
- User needs event marketing/promotion (use `atlas-marketing`)

## Workflow

### 1. Gather entertainment requirements
From event briefing, determine:
- **Event type:** corporate, gala, wedding, conference, festival, launch, party
- **Audience profile:** age range, demographics, cultural background, expectations
- **Event duration:** total hours requiring entertainment/programming
- **Key moments:** arrival, dinner, speeches, awards, dancing, transitions
- **Tone/energy:** formal, celebratory, educational, networking, party
- **Budget for entertainment:** percentage of total (typically 10-25%)
- **Venue constraints:** noise curfew, space, outdoor/indoor, neighbours
- **Theme:** if themed event, entertainment must align (see `atlas-decor`)

If no briefing exists, ask: event type, audience size/profile, duration, and budget range.

### 3. Programming design — the energy curve

#### Standard event energy flow
```
Energy
  ^
  |         **** PEAK (main entertainment/dancing)
  |       **    **
  |      *        *
  |     *          ** COOL DOWN
  |    * BUILD       *
  |   *                * FINALE
  | ** WELCOME          **
  |*________________________*______> Time
  Arrival                    End
```

#### Phase-by-phase programming

| Phase | Energy level | Entertainment type | Duration |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Arrival/welcome** | Low-medium | Solo musician, ambient DJ, roaming performer | 30-60min |
| **Opening** | Rising | MC welcome, short performance, brand moment | 10-15min |
| **Content/programme** | Medium | Speakers, panels, awards, ceremony | 60-180min |
| **Dinner** | Medium-low | Background music (trio, acoustic), close-up magic | 60-90min |
| **Build** | Rising | Band starts, tempo increases, transition acts | 30-45min |
| **Peak** | High | Full band/DJ, dance floor, high energy | 60-120min |
| **Cool down** | Descending | Slower songs, acoustic set, chill DJ | 15-30min |
| **Finale** | Emotional peak | Last song, fireworks, toast, group moment | 5-10min |

#### Variety and pacing rules
- Never have the same entertainment type for more than 90 minutes
- Alternate between passive (watching) and active (participating)
- Build audience engagement points every 20-30 minutes during content
- Allow transition time between acts (5-10 min for changeover)
- Schedule bio breaks/networking between content-heavy blocks

### 4. Artist management

#### Booking process
1. **Research and shortlist** — 3-5 options per entertainment slot
2. **Availability check** — date hold (typically 7-14 days without deposit)
3. **Budget negotiation** — fee, travel, accommodation, meals, extras
4. **Contract signing** — see contract essentials below
5. **Technical rider exchange** — share with `atlas-av` for technical planning
6. **Travel/logistics** — coordinate with `atlas-transport` and `atlas-accommodation`
7. **Pre-event communication** — briefing document 2 weeks before, final details 48h before
8. **Day-of management** — green room, sound check slot, set time, cue protocol

#### Contract essentials
Every performer/speaker contract must include:
- **Fee and payment terms:** amount, deposit (typically 50%), balance due date, payment method
- **Cancellation clause:** by client (forfeit deposit) vs. by artist (full refund + penalty)
- **Date, time, venue:** exact set times, sound check time, arrival time
- **Technical requirements:** reference to rider, who provides what
- **Travel/accommodation:** who pays, standards, distance limits
- **Exclusivity:** territorial or temporal exclusivity if required
- **Recording/streaming rights:** can the performance be streamed, recorded, shared?
- **Force majeure:** weather (outdoor), pandemic, venue closure
- **Insurance:** performer's public liability, equipment insurance

#### Green room requirements (standard)
- Private room near stage, lockable
- Mirror, good lighting, power outlets
- Water, light snacks, hot drinks
- Towels (for performers)
- Wi-Fi access
- Quiet — not next to PA or generator

### 5. MC/Host management

#### Role definition
The MC is the thread that ties the event together. Responsibilities:
- **Open the event:** welcome, set the tone, housekeeping
- **Transitions:** bridge between programme elements smoothly
- **Sponsor mentions:** contractual sponsor acknowledgements (see `atlas-sponsor`)
- **Audience interaction:** maintain energy, manage Q&A, handle dead time
- **Time management:** keep programme on schedule (coordinate with stage manager)
- **Crisis management:** fill time if delays, redirect if issues, maintain calm

#### MC briefing document
Provide to the MC minimum 1 week before:
- Full event run sheet with timings
- Key people (names, titles, pronunciation guide)
- Sponsor mentions (exact wording if contractually required)
- Stories/anecdotes approved by client
- Topics to avoid (sensitive subjects, competitor names)
- Audience profile and expected energy
- Emergency procedures and code words

### 6. Music licensing — SPA (Portugal)

#### Sociedade Portuguesa de Autores
- **Required for:** any public performance of copyrighted music (live or recorded)
- **Who pays:** event organiser (not the venue, not the performer)
- **Application:** online at spa.pt or via email, minimum 10 working days before event
- **Cost calculation:** based on capacity, ticket price (if applicable), event type
- **Typical costs:**
  - Private event (wedding, corporate, <200 pax): 80-300 EUR
  - Public event (<500 pax): 200-600 EUR
  - Public event (500-2,000 pax): 500-2,000 EUR
  - Festival/concert (2,000+ pax): 2,000-10,000+ EUR
- **Penalties for non-compliance:** fines up to 10x the licence fee
- **Exceptions:** original music only (no covers), spoken word, truly private events in private residences

### 7. Speaker management

#### Briefing document for speakers
Send minimum 2 weeks before event:
- **Event context:** theme, objectives, audience profile, expected outcomes
- **Presentation guidelines:** duration, format (16:9), branding templates, font sizes
- **Technical setup:** screen size, aspect ratio, clicker provided, confidence monitor, teleprompter
- **AV requirements:** submit to production team via `atlas-av`
- **Content deadlines:** final presentation due 48h before (for backup loading and tech check)
- **Q&A format:** moderated, open floor, written questions, duration
- **Photography/recording consent:** will the session be photographed, streamed, recorded?

#### Q&A moderation protocol
- Moderator introduces format at session start (how to ask, time limit per question)
- Written questions (Slido, cards) for large audiences — pre-filter
- Roaming mic for floor questions — AV must have wireless ready
- Time warning to speaker: 5min, 2min, 0min (via confidence monitor or cue card)
- Moderator closes Q&A and transitions to next session — never let it overrun

#### Backup speaker protocol
- For critical sessions (keynote, VIP panel), identify a backup speaker
- Backup must be briefed and available (not necessarily on site, but reachable)
- Alternative: pre-recorded video message from speaker as emergency fallback
- MC prepared to extend or rearrange programme if speaker cancels last-minute

### 9. Children's entertainment

#### Safety and supervision
- **Supervision ratios:** 1 adult per 4 children (under 5), 1:8 (ages 5-12)
- **Separate area:** enclosed, safe, visible to parents, near WC
- **Background checks:** all entertainment staff working with children — criminal record check mandatory
- **First aid:** trained staff or nurse on site for children's area
- **Allergies/medical:** parent registration with allergy/medical info
- **Check-in/out system:** numbered wristbands, parent-only pickup

#### Age-appropriate entertainment
| Age group | Activities | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| 0-3 | Soft play, sensory, bubbles | Parent supervision required |
| 3-6 | Face painting, balloon artist, storytelling | Short attention span, variety |
| 6-10 | Games, crafts, mini disco, magic show | Active, competitive, group |
| 10-14 | Gaming zone, photo booth, challenges | Independence, tech-savvy |

#### Cultural calendar awareness
- **January:** Dia de Reis (6 Jan) — Bolo Rei, Janeiras singing groups
- **February-March:** Carnaval — costume party, samba, desfile
- **March-April:** Pascoa — traditional, family-oriented
- **June:** Santos Populares (Santo Antonio 13 Jun, Sao Joao 24 Jun, Sao Pedro 29 Jun) — arraial, marchas, sardines
- **Summer:** Festas de Verao, beach events, outdoor festivals
- **October:** Vindimas (grape harvest) — wine events, harvest celebrations

- **December:** Natal — corporate events, galas, festive entertainment

### 11. Cultural sensitivity

- **Language:** if international audience, entertainment should be bilingual or language-neutral (music, visual acts)
- **Religious events:** appropriate music and performance content (no profanity, respectful repertoire)
- **Corporate events:** check company guidelines on alcohol, entertainment style, dress code
- **Accessibility:** sign language interpreter for deaf attendees, audio description for blind, wheelchair-accessible performance areas
- **Volume and timing:** respect audience age and event type (gala guests over 50 do not want 110dB at dinner)

## Red flags

- **No SPA music licence:** performing copyrighted music publicly without a licence triggers fines up to 10x the fee — apply minimum 10 working days before the event
- **No written contract with performer:** verbal agreements lead to disputes over fee, set time, cancellation, and recording rights — always execute a contract before paying any deposit
- **Sound check time conflict with setup:** sound check requires a quiet venue; if catering is setting tables or decor is being installed simultaneously, the sound check is worthless — coordinate via `atlas-timeline`
- **Single entertainer for 4+ hour event:** one performer for the entire evening leads to fatigue and audience boredom — rotate acts or book longer sets with breaks
- **No backup plan for performer cancellation:** illness, travel disruption, and no-shows happen — identify a replacement option for every critical entertainment slot
- **Energy curve ignored:** programming a high-energy DJ set during dinner or a slow acoustic set when the dance floor should be peaking kills the event flow — design the energy curve deliberately
- **Inappropriate content for audience:** comedy, music lyrics, and interactive games must match the audience (corporate vs. social, age, culture) — brief all performers on boundaries
- **No briefing document for MC:** an unbriefed MC will mispronounce names, miss sponsor mentions, and lose control of timing — provide a detailed brief minimum 1 week before
- **Children's entertainment without supervision ratios:** 1 entertainer with 40 children and no assistants is unsafe and ineffective — enforce ratios
- **Fado during a high-energy party:** Fado is intimate and contemplative; programming it at midnight after a DJ set creates jarring tonal dissonance — use it during dinner or as an opening cultural moment

## Integration with other atlas-* skills

- **atlas-av** — all entertainment has technical requirements (sound, lighting, staging); technical riders must be shared with AV production team early
- **atlas-staging** — performer requirements (stage size, wings, reveal mechanisms, drum riser) inform stage design decisions
- **atlas-timeline** — entertainment set times, sound checks, and changeovers must be in the master timeline
- **atlas-budget** — entertainment is typically 10-25% of total event budget; provide itemized breakdown
- **atlas-decor** — themed entertainment must visually align with event decoration and ambiance
- **atlas-catering** — dinner service timing must coordinate with entertainment (no clatter during speeches, no service during peak performance)
- **atlas-sponsor** — sponsor mentions during MC segments, branded entertainment moments, sponsored activities
- **atlas-transport** — artist travel logistics, equipment transport
- **atlas-accommodation** — artist hotel bookings, green room as temporary space
- **atlas-compliance** — SPA licensing, IGAC requirements for public performances, noise regulations
- **atlas-risk** — performer cancellation, weather (outdoor), audience safety during performances
