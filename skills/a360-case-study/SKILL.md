# A360 Case Study Generator

## Triggers

Activate this skill when the user says any of:
- "case study", "caso de estudo", "case study generator"
- "client story", "historia do cliente"
- "before and after", "antes e depois"
- "testimonial", "testemunho"
- "portfolio", "portfolio piece"
- "success story", "historia de sucesso"
- "document this win", "documentar esta vitoria"
- Any request to document a client result, transformation, or success for marketing purposes

## Frameworks & References

- **Donald Miller** (StoryBrand) — SB7 framework: character, problem, guide, plan, CTA, success, failure
- **Russell Brunson** (DotCom Secrets) — Epiphany Bridge, origin story, transformation narrative
- **Robert Cialdini** (Influence) — social proof, authority, consistency
- **Alex Hormozi** ($100M Offers) — results-first marketing, proof stacking
- **Andy Raskin** — strategic narrative for sales (5-part story structure)
- **Chip & Dan Heath** (Made to Stick) — SUCCESs: Simple, Unexpected, Concrete, Credible, Emotional, Stories

### Step 2: Transformation Narrative (StoryBrand + Epiphany Bridge)

Structure the story in 7 beats:

| Beat | StoryBrand Element | Your Case Study |
|------|-------------------|-----------------|
| 1 | **Character** (hero = client) | "[Client] is a [description] who wanted [desire]..." |
| 2 | **Problem** (external + internal + philosophical) | "But they struggled with [pain]. They felt [emotion]. It wasn't fair because [injustice]..." |
| 3 | **Guide** (you) | "That's when they found [your company/service]..." |
| 4 | **Plan** (your process) | "We created a clear plan: Step 1... Step 2... Step 3..." |
| 5 | **Call to Action** | "They decided to commit to [your offer]..." |
| 6 | **Success** (transformation) | "Within [timeframe], they achieved [specific results]..." |
| 7 | **Failure Avoided** | "Without this, they would have continued to [consequence]..." |

### Step 4: Testimonial Extraction

**How to Ask for Testimonials (3 Methods):**

**Method 1: The Guided Interview (Best Quality)**
Ask these questions, record the answers:
1. "What was your situation before working with us?"
2. "What almost stopped you from signing up?"
3. "What specific results have you seen?"
4. "What surprised you most about the experience?"
5. "What would you say to someone on the fence?"
6. "How has this changed your business/life?"

**Method 2: The Fill-in-the-Blank**
"Before working with [Company], I was struggling with _____. I was skeptical because _____. But after [timeframe], I've achieved _____. The best part is _____. I'd recommend this to anyone who _____."

**Method 3: The Screenshot Request**
"Could you share a quick message/screenshot about your experience? Even a few lines in a DM or email would be amazing."

**Testimonial Types to Collect:**
| Type | Format | Use Case | Impact |
|------|--------|----------|--------|
| **Written quote** | Text, 2-4 sentences | Website, sales page, email | Medium |
| **Video testimonial** | 30-90 second video | Sales page, social, ads | Very High |
| **Screenshot** | DM, email, chat screenshot | Social proof, ads | High |
| **Data/metric** | Graph, dashboard screenshot | Pitch deck, case study | Very High |
| **Social post** | Public post mentioning you | Social proof, reshare | High |
| **Review** | Google, Trustpilot, G2 | SEO, trust signals | High |

### Step 5: Case Study Formats

**Format 1: Long-Form (Website/Blog) — 800-1500 words**
1. Title: "[Client] increased [metric] by [X%] in [timeframe]"
2. Client snapshot (industry, size, challenge)
3. The challenge (2-3 paragraphs, emotional)
4. The solution (your process, 3-5 steps)
5. The results (metrics, quotes, screenshots)
6. Key takeaways (3 learnings)
7. CTA: "Want similar results? [link]"

**Format 2: One-Pager (PDF/Sales) — 1 page**
1. Client logo + quote
2. Challenge (3 bullet points)
3. Solution (3 bullet points)
4. Results (3 metrics with before/after)
5. CTA

**Format 3: Social Proof Snippet — 100-200 words**
"[Client] came to us with [problem]. In [timeframe], we [solution]. Result: [metric] went from [X] to [Y]. Here's what they said: '[quote]'"

**Format 4: Video Script — 2-3 minutes**
1. Hook: "What if you could [result] in [timeframe]?" (5s)
2. Meet [client]: background and challenge (30s)
3. The turning point: finding your solution (20s)
4. The process: what happened step by step (45s)
5. The results: specific metrics and transformation (30s)
6. Client testimonial: in their own words (20s)
7. CTA: how to get started (10s)

**Format 5: Slide Deck Case Study — 5-7 slides**
| Slide | Content |
|-------|---------|
| 1 | Title + key metric highlight |
| 2 | Client background + challenge |
| 3 | Solution approach (your process) |
| 4 | Results (metrics, charts) |
| 5 | Testimonial quote |
| 6 | Key takeaways |
| 7 | CTA / Contact |

### Step 7: Quality Scoring

Rate the case study effectiveness:

| Criterion | Score (1-10) | Notes |
|-----------|-------------|-------|
| **Specificity** — Real names, numbers, dates | /10 | |
| **Relatability** — Target audience sees themselves | /10 | |
| **Transformation** — Clear before/after contrast | /10 | |
| **Credibility** — Verifiable, not exaggerated | /10 | |
| **Emotion** — Reader FEELS something | /10 | |
| **Actionability** — Clear CTA at the end | /10 | |
| **Metrics** — Quantified results (not vague) | /10 | |
| **Visuals** — Screenshots, charts, photos | /10 | |
| **TOTAL** | **/80** | |

- 65-80: Excellent — publish everywhere
- 50-64: Good — publish with minor improvements
- 35-49: Fair — needs more data or better narrative
- 0-34: Weak — go back and collect more information

## Red Flags

Stop and warn the user if:
- No permission from client to publish (legal risk)
- Results are exaggerated or fabricated (reputation destruction)
- Case study is vague with no specific metrics ("things improved" means nothing)
- Using client data without anonymization when required
- Before state is missing (no contrast = no impact)
- No testimonial from the actual client (just your perspective)
- Case study reads like a brochure instead of a story
- No clear CTA — case study is interesting but doesn't convert
- Publishing a case study where the client later churned (bad optics)
- All case studies look identical (no variety in stories/industries)
