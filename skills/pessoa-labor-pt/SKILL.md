## Workflow

### Step 1: Contract Types

| Contract Type | PT Name | Duration | Renewal | Trial Period | Best For |
|---------------|---------|----------|---------|-------------|----------|
| **Permanent** | Sem termo | Indefinite | N/A | 90 days (general) / 180 days (management/complex) / 15 days (<6mo term) | Standard employment |
| **Fixed-term** | Termo certo | Max 2 years | Max 3 renewals, total max 2 years | 15-30 days | Temporary needs, seasonal |
| **Uncertain-term** | Termo incerto | Until event/task ends (max 4 years) | N/A | 15-30 days | Project-based, replacement |
| **Part-time** | Tempo parcial | Any | Per base contract type | Per base contract type | Reduced hours |
| **Intermittent** | Intermitente | Indefinite, alternating active/inactive | N/A | Per standard rules | Seasonal businesses |
| **Telework** | Teletrabalho | Any (added clause) | Per base contract type | Per base contract type | Remote work |

**Contract Formalization:**
- Written contract required within 60 days (Art. 110 CT)
- Must include: parties, workplace, role description, salary, working hours, start date, trial period
- Employer must communicate to SS before start date
- Copy to employee on day 1

### Step 2: Working Hours & Schedules

| Element | Standard Rule | Exceptions |
|---------|-------------|-----------|
| **Normal weekly hours** | 40 hours | Collective agreements may reduce |
| **Normal daily hours** | 8 hours | Adaptability regime allows variation |
| **Maximum daily** | 10 hours (including overtime) | Exceptions for force majeure |
| **Weekly rest** | Minimum 1 day (Sunday default) | Shift work, specific sectors |
| **Interval** | Min 1 hour, max 2 hours between work periods | Can be reduced to 30 min by agreement |
| **Night work** | 20:00 to 07:00 | +25% premium |

**Overtime Rules (Art. 226-231 CT):**

| Day Type | First Hour | Additional Hours | Annual Limit |
|----------|-----------|-----------------|-------------|
| **Workday** | +25% | +37.5% | 150 hours/year (200 for companies <250 employees) |
| **Rest day / Holiday** | +50% | +50% | Included in annual limit |

**Flexible Work Arrangements:**
- Banco de horas (time bank): up to 150 hours/year by individual agreement
- Horario concentrado: 4 days/week with longer days (max 10h/day)
- Isencao de horario: Exempt from fixed schedule (management roles)

### Step 3: Holidays, Leave & Absences

**Annual Leave (Ferias):**

| Element | Rule |
|---------|------|
| **Days per year** | 22 working days (minimum) |
| **First year** | 2 days per month after 6 months, usable after 6 months |
| **Scheduling** | By agreement; employer can set 1 period, employee the other |
| **Carry-over** | Can carry over to next year (must use by April 30) |
| **Sale of holidays** | Employee may sell up to 1/3 of days (with employer agreement) |
| **Subsidio de ferias** | Equal to base salary, paid before vacation starts |

**Public Holidays (Feriados obrigatorios):**
1 Jan, Sexta-feira Santa, Domingo de Pascoa, 25 Apr, 1 May, Corpo de Deus, 10 Jun, 15 Aug, 5 Oct, 1 Nov, 1 Dec, 8 Dec, 25 Dec + Municipal holiday (1 day)

**Justified Absences (Faltas justificadas, Art. 249 CT):**

| Reason | Duration | Paid? |
|--------|----------|-------|
| Marriage | 15 consecutive days | Yes |
| Death of spouse/parent/child | 5 consecutive days | Yes |
| Death of other family | 2 consecutive days | Yes |
| Prenatal appointments | As needed | Yes |
| Union activity | Per law | No (union responsibility) |
| Unavoidable force majeure | As needed | Depends |
| Blood donation | As needed | Yes |
| Legal proceedings | As needed | No (unless witness subpoena) |

### Step 4: Parental Leave (Parentalidade)

| Leave Type | Duration | Paid? | By Whom |
|-----------|----------|-------|---------|
| **Initial parental (mother)** | 120 days (100% pay) or 150 days (80% pay) | Yes | SS |
| **Shared parental** | +30 days if father takes 30+ days of initial leave | Yes | SS |
| **Mandatory father** | 28 working days (7 consecutive mandatory after birth + 21 more in first 6 weeks) | Yes | SS |
| **Breastfeeding** | 2 periods of 1h/day until child is 1 year | Yes | Employer |
| **Parental leave (extended)** | 3 months per parent until child is 6 | No (may have SS support) | SS |
| **Child illness** | Up to 30 days/year (child <12), 15 days (child >12) | Yes (65% by SS) | SS |
| **Adoption** | Same as biological parents | Yes | SS |

**Employer Obligations:**
- Cannot refuse parental leave
- Cannot discriminate based on parental status
- Must hold position (or equivalent) during leave
- Flexible schedule rights for parents of children under 12

### Step 5: Termination & Dismissal

**Termination Types:**

| Type | Initiative | Notice Period | Compensation | Procedure |
|------|-----------|---------------|-------------|-----------|
| **Mutual agreement** (acordo) | Both parties | As agreed | As agreed (min legal if employer initiates) | Written agreement |
| **Employee resignation** | Employee | 30 days (<2 years) / 60 days (2+ years) | None | Written notice |
| **Just cause** (justa causa) | Employer | None | None | Disciplinary procedure required |
| **Collective dismissal** | Employer | 15-75 days (by tenure) | Legal compensation | DGERT notification, worker consultation |
| **Position extinction** | Employer | 15-75 days | Legal compensation | Written communication, criteria compliance |
| **Inadaptation** | Employer | As per law | Legal compensation | Documented support provided first |
| **Trial period** | Either party | None | None | Written notice recommended |

**Compensation for Dismissal (indemnizacao):**
- 14 days of base salary + seniority per year of service (for contracts from 2013+)
- Minimum: 3 months base salary
- Varies by contract start date (pre-2011 rules differ)

**Just Cause Examples (Art. 351 CT):**
- Repeated unjustified absences
- Disobedience to legitimate orders
- Violence, harassment, or discrimination
- Theft or fraud
- Substance abuse affecting work
- Breach of confidentiality

**Disciplinary Procedure (mandatory for just cause):**
1. Nota de culpa (written charges) to employee
2. Employee has 10 working days to respond
3. Instruction phase (evidence gathering)
4. Workers' committee consulted (if exists)
5. Final decision communicated in writing
6. Employee can challenge in court (within 60 days)

### Step 6: IRCT (Collective Agreements)

| Instrument | Description | Scope |
|-----------|-------------|-------|
| **CCT** (Convencao Coletiva de Trabalho) | Agreement between employer association and union | Sector/industry |
| **AE** (Acordo de Empresa) | Agreement with specific company | Single company |
| **PE** (Portaria de Extensao) | Government extension of CCT to non-signatory parties | Extended sector |

**IRCT Impact on HR:**
- May set higher minimum salaries than national SMN
- May define additional benefits (diuturnidades, extra holidays)
- May set specific working hour rules
- Always check applicable IRCT before setting compensation

**Find applicable IRCT**: DGERT portal (dgert.gov.pt) or BTE (Boletim do Trabalho e Emprego)

### Step 7: Key Compliance Checklist

| Obligation | Deadline | Entity | Penalty for Non-Compliance |
|-----------|----------|--------|--------------------------|
| Written contract | Within 60 days of start | Employer | EUR 204-9,690 |
| SS registration (employee) | Before start date | Seguranca Social | EUR 500-12,500 |
| Work accident insurance | Before start date | Insurance company | Criminal liability |
| Training (40h/year) | Annual | Employer | EUR 204-9,690 |
| Vacation scheduling | By April 15 | Employer | EUR 204-4,080 |
| DMR (monthly SS declaration) | By day 10 of following month | SS | Late fees + penalties |
| Annual activity report (Relatorio Unico) | By March/April | GEP/MTSSS | EUR 204-9,690 |
| Safety & health assessment | Before start of activity | Employer | EUR 2,040-61,200 |
| RGPD employee data processing | Ongoing | Employer/DPO | Up to EUR 20M or 4% revenue |

### Step 8: Common HR Legal Scenarios

| Scenario | Legal Answer | Reference |
|----------|-------------|-----------|
| "Can I fire during trial period?" | Yes, no justification needed, no compensation | Art. 114 CT |
| "Can I reduce salary?" | Only by mutual written agreement | Art. 129 CT |
| "Can I require overtime?" | Yes, within limits (150-200h/year), with premium | Art. 226 CT |
| "Can I monitor employee email?" | Very limited, must inform beforehand, proportional | Art. 22 CT + RGPD |
| "Can I prohibit remote work?" | Generally yes, unless employee has legal right (children <8, etc.) | Art. 166-A CT |
| "Must I pay subsidio alimentacao?" | Not legally required (unless IRCT says so), but market standard | N/A |
| "Can I hire a foreigner?" | Yes, with valid work permit/residence authorization | SEF/AIMA rules |

## Red Flags

Stop and warn the user if:
- Attempting to dismiss without required disciplinary procedure (illegal)
- No written contract after 60 days (presumed permanent contract)
- Missing work accident insurance (criminal liability for employer)
- Employee working beyond overtime limits without tracking
- Discrimination in hiring, pay, or promotion based on protected characteristics
- Parental leave denied or discouraged (CITE complaint risk)
- Training obligation not met (40h/year minimum)
- Employee monitoring without prior notification and proportionality
- Using fixed-term contracts to fill permanent needs (contract requalification risk)
- Salary below applicable IRCT minimum (even if above national SMN)
- No SS registration before start date (heavy penalties)
