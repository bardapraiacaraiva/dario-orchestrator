- **Spotify Model** — Squads, tribes, chapters, guilds (scaled agile)
- **Stanley McChrystal** (Team of Teams) — Shared consciousness, empowered execution

### Step 2: Structure Type Selection (Mintzberg)

| Structure | Best For | Pros | Cons | Size |
|-----------|----------|------|------|------|
| **Simple** | Startups, <15 people | Fast, flexible, clear | Founder bottleneck, fragile | 1-15 |
| **Functional** | Single-product, stable | Expertise depth, efficiency | Silos, slow cross-func | 15-100 |
| **Divisional** | Multi-product/market | Autonomy, accountability | Duplication, coordination | 100-500 |
| **Matrix** | Complex, multi-project | Flexibility, dual reporting | Confusion, conflict | 100+ |
| **Flat/Holacratic** | Creative, small | Empowerment, speed | Chaos at scale, unclear | 10-50 |
| **Network/Team of Teams** | Distributed, agile | Adaptable, resilient | Requires trust, hard to manage | Any |

### Step 6: RACI Matrix

**For key processes/decisions:**

| Activity/Decision | Person A | Person B | Person C | Person D |
|-------------------|----------|----------|----------|----------|
| [Activity 1] | R | A | C | I |
| [Activity 2] | I | R | A | C |
| [Activity 3] | C | I | R | A |

**RACI Key:**
- **R** = Responsible (does the work)
- **A** = Accountable (owns the outcome, only 1 per activity)
- **C** = Consulted (provides input before decision)
- **I** = Informed (notified after decision)

**Rules:**
- Every activity has exactly ONE "A"
- Minimize "C" and "I" (reduces bottlenecks)
- "R" and "A" can be the same person for simple tasks
- If everyone is "C", nobody is — reduce consultation

### Step 7: Restructuring Playbook

**When to Restructure:**
- Business strategy changed significantly
- Growth requires new capabilities or departments
- Silos preventing cross-functional collaboration
- Key roles unclear, causing conflicts or gaps
- Merger, acquisition, or significant market shift

**Restructuring Process:**

| Phase | Duration | Activities |
|-------|----------|-----------|
| **1. Diagnose** | 2-4 weeks | Assess current state, identify pain points, gather data |
| **2. Design** | 2-4 weeks | Create new structure options, evaluate trade-offs |
| **3. Decide** | 1-2 weeks | Leadership alignment, select structure, plan communication |
| **4. Communicate** | 1 week | All-hands announcement, 1:1 conversations, Q&A |
| **5. Implement** | 4-8 weeks | Role transitions, reporting changes, system updates |
| **6. Stabilize** | 8-12 weeks | Monitor, adjust, support, measure effectiveness |

**Communication Plan:**

| Audience | When | By Whom | Channel | Key Message |
|----------|------|---------|---------|-------------|
| Leadership team | Day -7 | CEO | In-person meeting | Full details, rationale, role in communication |
| Affected employees | Day 0 | Direct managers | 1:1 conversations | Personal impact, support available |
| All employees | Day 0 | CEO | All-hands / email | Vision, rationale, timeline |
| External stakeholders | Day +1 | CEO/Comms | Email / call | Continuity assurance |

### Step 8: Team Topology Design (Skelton & Pais)

| Team Type | Purpose | Size | Example |
|-----------|---------|------|---------|
| **Stream-aligned** | Delivers value in a business domain | 5-9 | Product squad, client team |
| **Platform** | Provides shared services/tools | 3-7 | DevOps, internal tools |
| **Enabling** | Helps other teams adopt new capabilities | 2-5 | L&D, coaching, architecture |
| **Complicated-subsystem** | Manages complex components | 3-7 | Data science, ML, compliance |

**Interaction Modes:**
- **Collaboration**: Close working together (temporary, time-boxed)
- **X-as-a-Service**: Clear API/interface between teams
- **Facilitating**: One team helps another improve (enabling)

## Red Flags

Stop and warn the user if:
- Span of control exceeds 12 for any manager (management capacity exceeded)
- More than 5 levels of hierarchy for a company under 100 people
- No RACI for key processes (conflict and confusion guaranteed)
- Restructuring announced without individual 1:1 conversations (trust destroyer)
- Matrix reporting without clear primary reporting line (confusion)
- Role definitions missing decision authority (creates bottlenecks)
- Restructuring driven by personality conflicts, not strategy
- No role for a departing executive but keeping the title (ghost roles)
- Every new initiative creates a new team (team sprawl)
- No transition support during restructuring (productivity collapse)
