### Step 2: AARRR Pirate Metrics Audit (McClure/Ellis)

Score each stage 1-10 and identify the biggest leak:

| Stage | Metric | Current | Benchmark | Score | Priority |
|-------|--------|---------|-----------|-------|----------|
| **Acquisition** | Visitors/leads per month | X | X | /10 | |
| **Activation** | % who complete first value action | X% | >25% | /10 | |
| **Retention** | % who return in 30 days | X% | >40% | /10 | |
| **Revenue** | Conversion to paying | X% | >2% | /10 | |
| **Referral** | % who refer 1+ person | X% | >15% | /10 | |

**Growth Bottleneck**: The stage with the LOWEST score is your priority.
Fix the bottleneck BEFORE trying to scale acquisition.

### Step 3: Growth Loop Design (Balfour)

A growth loop is a system where the output of one cycle becomes the input of the next:

**Viral Loop:**
```
User signs up → User gets value → User invites others → New users sign up → [loop]
```

**Content Loop:**
```
Create content → Content ranks/spreads → Visitors arrive → Some convert → Revenue funds more content → [loop]
```

**Paid Loop:**
```
Spend $X on ads → Acquire customers → Revenue > $X → Reinvest profit into ads → [loop]
```

**UGC Loop:**
```
Users create content → Content attracts new users → New users create content → [loop]
```

Design your PRIMARY growth loop:

| Element | Your Business |
|---------|--------------|
| **Input** | What starts the loop? |
| **Action** | What does the user do? |
| **Output** | What do they produce? |
| **Reinvestment** | How does output fuel new input? |
| **Cycle time** | How long is one loop? |
| **Amplification** | Does each cycle grow or stay flat? |

### Step 4: Viral Coefficient & Mechanics

**Viral Coefficient (K-factor):**
K = invitations per user x conversion rate of invitations

- K > 1.0 = Viral growth (each user brings more than 1 new user)
- K = 0.5-1.0 = Amplified growth (helps reduce CAC)
- K < 0.5 = Low virality (need paid/organic to grow)

**Viral Mechanics to Implement:**

| Mechanism | Description | K-factor Impact | Effort |
|-----------|-------------|-----------------|--------|
| **Referral program** | Give X, get Y for each referral | High | Medium |
| **Social sharing** | Share results/achievements publicly | Medium | Low |
| **Collaboration** | Product requires inviting others | Very High | High |
| **Word of mouth** | Product is so good people talk about it | Medium | Low (product-driven) |
| **Embedded virality** | "Powered by [Brand]" on outputs | Medium | Low |
| **Community** | Users form groups, invite others | High | Medium |
| **Affiliate program** | Pay commission for referrals | High | Medium |

### Step 5: Referral Program Design

| Element | Your Program |
|---------|-------------|
| **Trigger** | When to ask for referral (after first success moment) |
| **Incentive (referrer)** | What they get: credit, free month, cash, feature unlock |
| **Incentive (referee)** | What new person gets: discount, bonus, trial extension |
| **Mechanism** | Unique link, code, email invite, social share |
| **Double-sided** | Both parties benefit (critical for conversion) |
| **Tracking** | How to attribute referrals |
| **Communication** | Email/in-app prompt sequence |

**Referral Program Benchmarks:**
- Participation rate (% of customers who refer): target >15%
- Conversion rate (% of referred who sign up): target >10%
- Time to referral (days after purchase): track and optimize

### Step 6: Content Flywheel

Build a self-reinforcing content engine:

```
Pillar Content (1x/week)
    ↓ repurpose into
Social Posts (5x/week) + Email Newsletter (1x/week)
    ↓ drives
Traffic + Email List Growth
    ↓ converts to
Customers + Revenue
    ↓ funds
More Content Production + Tools + Guests
    ↓ creates
More Pillar Content [LOOP]
```

**Content Distribution Matrix:**

| Platform | Format | Frequency | Goal |
|----------|--------|-----------|------|
| Blog/Website | Long-form article | 1-2x/week | SEO, authority |
| YouTube | Video | 1x/week | Discovery, trust |
| LinkedIn | Post/carousel | 3-5x/week | B2B reach |
| Instagram | Reel + stories | 5-7x/week | B2C reach |
| Twitter/X | Thread + posts | Daily | Thought leadership |
| Email | Newsletter | 1-2x/week | Nurture, convert |
| Podcast | Episode | 1x/week | Deep trust |

**1-to-10 Rule**: Every piece of pillar content should be repurposed into at least 10 pieces across platforms.

### Step 8: Paid Amplification Strategy

Scale what already works organically:

| Phase | Budget | Focus | Expected CAC |
|-------|--------|-------|-------------|
| **Test** ($10-30/day) | $300-900/mo | Test 5+ audiences and creatives | Finding baseline |
| **Optimize** ($30-100/day) | $900-3K/mo | Kill losers, scale winners | Reducing CAC |
| **Scale** ($100-500/day) | $3K-15K/mo | Horizontal + vertical scaling | Maintaining CAC |

**Scaling Rules:**
- Only scale ads with proven positive ROAS (>2:1)
- Increase budget max 20% every 3 days (avoid algorithm reset)
- Diversify: don't put >70% budget on one platform
- Always have 2-3 creatives in testing
- Retarget at every funnel stage

### Step 9: Retention Engine (Nir Eyal Hook Model)

| Element | Your Implementation |
|---------|-------------------|
| **Trigger** (external) | Email, push notification, social prompt |
| **Trigger** (internal) | Emotion, habit, need that brings them back |
| **Action** | Simplest behavior in anticipation of reward |
| **Variable Reward** | Social, self, hunt (unpredictable value) |
| **Investment** | User puts something in (data, content, reputation) |

**Retention Tactics:**

| Tactic | Type | Impact | Effort |
|--------|------|--------|--------|
| Onboarding optimization | Activation | Very High | Medium |
| Email drip sequences | Re-engagement | High | Low |
| Community/group | Social lock-in | Very High | Medium |
| Gamification / streaks | Habit formation | High | Medium |
| Regular product updates | Fresh value | Medium | Ongoing |
| Customer success check-ins | Personal touch | Very High | High |
| Loyalty / rewards program | Incentive | Medium | Medium |
| Exit surveys + win-back | Recovery | Medium | Low |

## Red Flags

Stop and warn the user if:
- Trying to scale acquisition before fixing activation/retention (leaky bucket)
- No North Star Metric defined (growing blindly)
- Spending on ads with unproven funnel economics (burning cash)
- Zero organic growth plan (100% paid = fragile)
- No referral mechanism in a product that people like (leaving growth on the table)
- Content plan without distribution strategy (creating content nobody sees)
- Growth experiments without clear success criteria (vanity metrics)
- Viral coefficient below 0.2 and no plan to increase it
- Retention curve flattens at zero (total churn, no product-market fit)
