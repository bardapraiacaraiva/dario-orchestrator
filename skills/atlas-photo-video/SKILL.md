# ATLAS Skill — Photography & Videography Coordination

Definitive reference for planning, coordinating, and managing all photography and videography at events — from shot lists and team sizing through rights management and RGPD compliance. Covers the full lifecycle from pre-event planning through same-day delivery and long-term content strategy, with deep knowledge of Portuguese copyright law, drone regulations, and data protection requirements.

## When to activate

Invoke `/atlas-photo-video` (or trigger automatically) when:
- User needs to plan photography or videography for an event
- User asks about shot lists, photographer quantities, or coverage hours
- User needs drone footage planning (ANAC regulations)
- User asks about live streaming or multi-camera recording
- User needs photo booth or social media content strategy
- User asks about photography rights, usage licences, or RGPD/GDPR compliance
- User mentions "fotografo", "video do evento", "drone", "fotos", "redes sociais"
- After `atlas-briefing` defines the event and before `atlas-timeline` locks the schedule

Do NOT use when:
- User needs only AV/production video (LED walls, IMAG) — use `atlas-av`
- User needs only social media marketing/ads strategy — use `atlas-marketing`
- User needs only photo opportunity design (photo wall) — use `atlas-decor` (but coordinate)

## Workflow

### 1. Gather photo/video requirements
From event briefing, determine:
- **Event type:** conference, gala, wedding, corporate, launch, festival
- **Audience size:** determines number of photographers/videographers
- **Key moments:** list of must-capture moments
- **Deliverables needed:** photos, highlight video, full recording, live stream, social content
- **Turnaround:** same-day selects, 48h gallery, 2-week video — define deadlines
- **Usage:** internal, social media, press, advertising, website, archive
- **Brand/sponsor requirements:** specific shots needed (logos, activations, signage)
- **RGPD considerations:** consent method, children present, opt-out process
- **Budget tier:** basic (1 photographer) / standard (photo + video) / premium (full production)

If no briefing exists, ask: event type, audience size, key moments, and deliverables needed.

### 2. Shot list — the definitive checklist

#### Must-have shots (non-negotiable)
**Pre-event / setup:**
- Venue empty (wide, details)
- Setup in progress (time-lapse if possible)
- Table settings, decor details, centrepieces, signage
- Stage/scenic before lights
- Name badges, programmes, branded materials
- Food/catering display before guests

**Arrivals and reception:**
- Exterior venue shot with signage
- First guests arriving
- Welcome/registration area
- VIP arrivals (pre-identified list with photos)
- Cocktail/networking candids

**Key moments:**
- Opening speech / MC welcome
- Every speaker/presenter (wide + close-up)
- Awards / prize-giving (winner receiving, reaction, group)
- Entertainment/performances
- Toasts / special moments
- Audience reactions (engaged, laughing, applauding)
- Standing ovation or group moments

**Candids and atmosphere:**
- Networking conversations
- Guests at tables (every table at least once)
- Bar/drinks service
- Dance floor
- Laughing/emotional moments
- Behind-the-scenes (crew, backstage)

**Group shots:**
- Full group (if possible, plan timing and location)
- Team/department photos (corporate)
- VIP group
- Speakers group
- Award winners group

**Brand and sponsor:**
- Every sponsor logo/banner (contractual — see `atlas-sponsor`)
- Branded moments (step-and-repeat, photo wall)
- Sponsor activations
- Product displays

**Closing:**
- Finale moment
- Departure/farewell
- Venue post-event (empty, lights on)

#### Nice-to-have shots
- Aerial/drone (if permitted and licensed)
- Time-lapse of full event
- Detail macro shots (flowers, food, textures)
- Behind-the-scenes of crew
- Guest selfies at photo opportunities

### 3. Photography planning

#### Team sizing
| Audience size | Event type | Photographers | Coverage hours |
|---|---|---|---|
| <50 | Corporate/social | 1 | 3-5h |
| 50-150 | Conference/gala | 1-2 | 4-8h |
| 150-300 | Conference/gala | 2 | 6-10h |
| 300-500 | Large conference | 2-3 | 8-12h |
| 500+ | Festival/congress | 3-5 | Full day+ |

For multi-room conferences: 1 photographer per active room minimum.

#### Photography style guide
| Style | Description | Best for |
|---|---|---|
| Documentary/reportage | Candid, unposed, storytelling | Conferences, networking, natural |
| Editorial/creative | Styled, composed, artistic | Galas, luxury, brand campaigns |
| Corporate | Clean, professional, posed when needed | Corporate events, headshots |
| Lifestyle | Warm, approachable, candid-styled | Social events, lifestyle brands |

#### Technical specifications for photographers
- Deliver in: RAW + processed JPEG (300dpi, sRGB for web; Adobe RGB for print)
- Minimum resolution: 24MP (full-frame preferred)
- Naming convention: EventName_YYYYMMDD_Photographer_NNNN.jpg
- Backup: dual card slots, backup to separate drive on site
- Consistent editing: Lightroom preset agreed with client before event

### 5. Live streaming

#### Setup requirements
- **Multi-camera switching:** minimum 2 cameras, video switcher (ATEM Mini or similar)
- **Graphics overlay:** lower thirds, sponsor logos, title cards — prepared in advance
- **Technical director:** dedicated person for switching, graphics, stream monitoring
- **Platform management:** YouTube Live, Vimeo, custom RTMP — test 24h before
- **Bandwidth:** dedicated wired connection, minimum 5-10 Mbps upload per stream (see `atlas-av`)
- **Backup stream:** secondary encoder to different platform/server
- **Latency monitoring:** 10-30 second latency typical, plan for this in interactive Q&A

#### Streaming quality tiers
| Tier | Resolution | Bitrate | Use case |
|---|---|---|---|
| Basic | 720p | 2.5-4 Mbps | Internal meetings, small audience |
| Standard | 1080p | 5-8 Mbps | Conferences, webinars |
| Premium | 1080p60 or 4K | 8-25 Mbps | Broadcast quality, large audience |

#### Photo booth best practices
- Position in high-traffic area (not hidden in a corner)
- Dedicated lighting (ring light or studio setup)
- Branded overlay/frame on all outputs
- Props box: themed, on-brand, fun but tasteful
- Instant sharing: QR code to download, email, or social media
- Attendant: at least 1 person to assist and maintain flow
- Data collection: optional email capture (RGPD compliant — see below)

### 7. Social media content strategy

#### Real-time posting plan
- **Designated social media coordinator:** not the photographer — separate role
- **Content calendar:** pre-planned post times aligned with programme moments
- **Same-day selects:** photographer delivers 5-10 edited photos within 4h for social posting
- **Platform-specific formats:**
  - Instagram Stories: vertical (9:16), 15-second clips, behind-the-scenes
  - Instagram Feed/Reels: square (1:1) or vertical (4:5), polished
  - LinkedIn: horizontal (16:9), professional tone, speaker quotes
  - TikTok: vertical (9:16), trending audio, authentic/raw feel
  - X/Twitter: horizontal, quote graphics, key takeaways

#### Hashtag management
- Create event-specific hashtag (short, unique, memorable)
- Display hashtag prominently: screens, signage, photo booth, printed materials
- Monitor hashtag in real-time (social media wall, TweetDeck)
- Curate user-generated content for reposting (with credit)

### 9. Rights and usage

#### Portuguese copyright law (Codigo de Direitos de Autor e Direitos Conexos)
- **Default:** photographer owns copyright to their images (unless contract states otherwise)
- **Work-for-hire:** not automatic in PT law — must be explicitly stated in contract
- **Usage licence:** define scope clearly — social media, website, press, advertising, duration, territory
- **Moral rights:** photographer retains right to be credited (cannot be waived in PT law)
- **Right to image (Article 79):** photographed individuals have rights over their image

#### Contract clauses for photographers/videographers
Every contract must specify:
- **Copyright ownership vs. licence:** who owns the files, what usage is granted
- **Usage scope:** platforms, media, duration, territory, exclusivity
- **Delivery format:** resolution, file types, naming, gallery platform
- **Deliverables and timeline:** what, when, how many
- **Editing/retouching:** number of retouched images included, revision rounds
- **Raw files:** if client receives RAW (usually extra cost or not at all)
- **Credits:** how and where photographer must be credited
- **Model releases:** who is responsible for obtaining (usually client)
- **Exclusivity:** can photographer use images in their portfolio? (usually yes with approval)
- **Cancellation/postponement:** terms and fees

### 10. RGPD/GDPR compliance for event photography

#### Consent mechanisms
- **Legitimate interest:** may apply for corporate events where photography is expected — but document the assessment
- **Consent at registration:** tick box during event registration ("I consent to being photographed...")
- **Signage at venue:** visible signs at all entrances stating photography/videography is taking place
- **Opt-out wristband/badge:** coloured wristband or badge mark indicating "do not photograph" — brief photographers
- **Children:** explicit parental/guardian written consent required — never photograph unidentified children

#### Required signage (Portuguese text template)
```
AVISO — CAPTACAO DE IMAGEM E VIDEO
Este evento esta a ser fotografado e filmado.
As imagens poderao ser utilizadas para divulgacao nas redes sociais,
website e materiais promocionais do evento.
Se nao deseja ser fotografado, solicite uma pulseira de exclusao na recepcao.
Nos termos do RGPD, pode exercer os seus direitos contactando: [email/DPO]
```

#### RGPD best practices
- **Data subject rights:** anyone can request removal of their image — have a process
- **Data retention:** define how long photos/videos are stored (recommend 2 years, then review)
- **Third-party sharing:** if sharing with sponsors/partners, disclose this in consent
- **Social media wall:** user-generated content displayed on screens — curate, do not auto-display without review
- **Post-event gallery:** password-protected, not publicly indexed, with privacy notice

### 11. Drone regulations (Portugal — ANAC)

#### Registration and licensing
- **Drone registration:** mandatory for all drones (ANAC/EASA — UAS registration)
- **Pilot licence:** A1/A3 minimum (online exam), A2 for closer-to-people flights
- **Specific category (STS):** required for most event scenarios (flying near people, urban areas)
- **Operator registration:** commercial operators must register with ANAC

#### Flight restrictions
- **No-fly zones:** airports (radius varies), military installations, national monuments, prisons, hospitals
- **Height limit:** 120m AGL (above ground level) maximum
- **Distance from people:** depends on category — minimum 30m horizontal in A2, further in A1/A3
- **Urban areas:** specific authorization required for most event locations
- **Night flights:** additional authorization required
- **Indoor flights:** EASA regulations do not apply indoors, but venue insurance may restrict

#### Event drone best practices
- **Flight plan:** submitted to ANAC minimum 10 working days before
- **Insurance:** mandatory third-party liability insurance for commercial operations
- **Weather check:** no-fly in rain, strong wind (>25 km/h for most consumer drones), fog
- **Noise:** drone noise during outdoor ceremonies or speeches is disruptive — schedule during setup or transitions
- **Spotter:** dedicated visual observer required in addition to pilot
- **Pre-flight briefing:** inform event security, venue, and nearby authorities

## Red flags

- **No shot list shared with photographers:** without a shot list, photographers guess — and miss critical moments (sponsor logos, VIP arrivals, group shots) that cannot be recreated
- **No RGPD signage at venue:** photographing attendees without proper notice and consent mechanism violates RGPD — fines up to 4% of turnover or 20M EUR
- **Unlicensed drone operator:** flying a drone at an event without ANAC registration, pilot licence, and insurance is illegal in Portugal and creates liability for the event organiser
- **Single photographer for 300+ guests:** one photographer cannot physically cover a large event with multiple spaces — guests, tables, and moments will be missed
- **No backup equipment:** camera failure with no backup body means zero coverage until resolved — mandate dual bodies or backup gear in contract
- **No written usage rights agreement:** without explicit written licence, the photographer owns all copyright and can restrict your use of the images — define this before the event
- **Photographer not briefed on VIP list:** without knowing who the VIPs are (with reference photos), the photographer cannot prioritise capturing them
- **Same-day edit promised without dedicated editor:** a same-day edit requires a dedicated editor working during the event, separate from the shooting team — it does not happen automatically
- **Social media wall without content moderation:** auto-displaying unmoderated user-generated content risks inappropriate or off-brand posts appearing on screen
- **No data retention policy for photos:** storing identifiable photos indefinitely without a defined retention period violates RGPD principles
