## Workflow

### Step 1: Engagement Measurement Framework

**Measurement Cadence:**

| Survey Type | Frequency | Length | Response Target | Purpose |
|-------------|-----------|--------|----------------|---------|
| **eNPS** | Monthly | 1 question + comment | >80% | Quick pulse on overall sentiment |
| **Pulse Survey** | Quarterly | 5-10 questions | >70% | Track engagement drivers |
| **Full Engagement Survey** | Annual | 30-50 questions | >75% | Deep diagnostic |
| **Stay Interview** | Semi-annual | 1:1 conversation | 100% (all employees) | Individual retention intel |
| **Exit Interview** | Per departure | Structured interview | 100% (all leavers) | Learn why people leave |

### Step 2: eNPS (Employee Net Promoter Score)

**The Question:**
"On a scale of 0-10, how likely are you to recommend [Company] as a place to work?"

**Follow-up:** "What is the primary reason for your score?"

**Scoring:**
- Promoters (9-10): Engaged, advocates
- Passives (7-8): Satisfied but not enthusiastic
- Detractors (0-6): Disengaged, risk of leaving

**eNPS = % Promoters - % Detractors**

| eNPS Range | Interpretation | Action |
|-----------|---------------|--------|
| 50+ | World-class | Maintain, celebrate, share externally |
| 30-49 | Excellent | Monitor, continuous improvement |
| 10-29 | Good | Identify improvement areas |
| 0-9 | Concerning | Investigate root causes urgently |
| Negative | Critical | Emergency intervention needed |

### Step 5: Stay Interview Template

**Conduct 1:1 with each employee (30 min, semi-annual):**

| Question | Purpose | Notes |
|----------|---------|-------|
| "What do you look forward to when you come to work each day?" | Identify engagement drivers | |
| "What are you learning here? What do you WANT to learn?" | Growth needs | |
| "Why do you stay at [Company]?" | Retention factors | |
| "When was the last time you thought about leaving? What prompted it?" | Risk identification | |
| "What would make your job more satisfying?" | Improvement opportunities | |
| "What talents are you NOT using in your current role?" | Underutilization | |
| "What can I do more of, or less of, as your manager?" | Manager development | |

**After Stay Interview:**
- Document key themes (anonymized for trends)
- Create individual action item (at least 1 per person)
- Follow up within 30 days on committed actions

### Step 7: Recognition Program Design

**Recognition Framework:**

| Type | Frequency | By Whom | Value | Example |
|------|-----------|---------|-------|---------|
| **Informal praise** | Daily | Anyone | Free | "Great work on X" in Slack/meeting |
| **Peer recognition** | Weekly | Peers | Free-EUR 25 | Shout-outs channel, kudos cards |
| **Manager spotlight** | Monthly | Manager | EUR 25-100 | Team meeting highlight + gift card |
| **Company award** | Quarterly | Leadership | EUR 100-500 | Values award ceremony |
| **Annual excellence** | Yearly | CEO/Board | EUR 500-2000 | Award dinner, trophy, bonus |
| **Milestone** | Per event | HR | EUR 50-200 | Work anniversary, certification |

**Recognition Rules:**
1. Specific (what they did), not generic ("good job")
2. Timely (within 48 hours of the behavior)
3. Public when appropriate (some prefer private)
4. Tied to values or outcomes
5. Equitable (track to avoid bias toward visible roles)

## Red Flags

Stop and warn the user if:
- eNPS negative for 2+ consecutive months (systemic problem)
- Survey response rate below 50% (results unreliable, trust issue)
- Survey results not shared with employees (breaks trust cycle)
- No action taken on survey results (survey fatigue, cynicism)
- Voluntary turnover above 25% annually (hemorrhaging talent)
- No stay interviews conducted (reactive instead of proactive)
- Recognition program only recognizes top performers (alienates majority)
- Manager engagement scores significantly below team average (manager problem)
- Exit interviews not analyzed for patterns (wasting data)
- Engagement efforts are purely events/perks without addressing root causes
- Zero budget for engagement initiatives

## 7. Status checklist per data point (Gate 7)

Every numeric value, name, or factual claim in **PESSOA Employee Engagement** output must carry an EXPLICIT label so the reader knows what to trust as-is and what needs verification before action.

- 🔵 **verified** — confirmed from a primary source (file, audit log, session memory, RAG hit, screen-shared confirmation)
- 🟡 **assumed** — plausible default that needs client/owner confirmation before being acted on
- 🟢 **projection** — estimate by design (timeline, cost, throughput, conversion); not verifiable today

**Why this gate exists:** outputs that mix verified facts with assumptions and projections without flagging them produce false certainty. The reader either over-trusts (acts on a guess) or under-trusts (ignores even the verified parts). Explicit labels restore signal.

**How to apply in PESSOA Employee Engagement:**

1. After drafting the deliverable, scan it for every concrete claim (number, name, date, metric, status, recommendation).
2. Attach one of the three labels inline; if you can't pick a label confidently, the claim isn't ready to ship.
3. Add a short citation in parentheses for 🔵 items (file path, source, dashboard) and a short condition for 🟡 / 🟢 items (what would confirm or refute it).
4. End the deliverable with a 1-line summary of how many items in each category, e.g. `Status mix: 8 🔵 · 3 🟡 · 2 🟢`.

❌ **NOT delivery-ready:**

```
Conversion rate is 18%. CAC is R$ 420. We will hit 1k MAU in Q3.
```

✅ **Delivery-ready:**

```
- Conversion rate: 18% 🔵 verified (Mixpanel funnel report 2026-05-19, n=1,242 sessions)
- CAC: R$ 420 🟡 assumed (calculated from May spend ÷ May customers; CFO has not signed off yet)
- 1k MAU in Q3 🟢 projection (linear extrapolation of last 8 weeks; assumes no churn spike)

Status mix: 1 🔵 · 1 🟡 · 1 🟢
```

**Ship checklist post-cliente-sync:**
- [ ] All 🟡 items confirmed (or downgraded to 🟢 / dropped)
- [ ] All 🔵 citations actually exist (no broken file paths, no imagined sources)
- [ ] All 🟢 projections labeled as such to the client — never presented as commitments
