## Workflow

### Step 1: Wellbeing Assessment

**Maslach 6 Areas of Worklife:**

| Area | Healthy Signs | Warning Signs | Score (1-5) |
|------|-------------|---------------|-------------|
| **Workload** | Manageable, clear priorities | Chronic overload, no boundaries | /5 |
| **Control** | Autonomy, decision authority | Micromanagement, no input | /5 |
| **Reward** | Fair pay, recognition, growth | Underpaid, invisible, stuck | /5 |
| **Community** | Trust, collaboration, belonging | Isolation, conflict, politics | /5 |
| **Fairness** | Transparent, equitable | Favoritism, inconsistency | /5 |
| **Values** | Meaningful work, aligned values | Ethical conflicts, purposeless | /5 |

**Burnout Risk Assessment:**

| Indicator | Low Risk | Medium Risk | High Risk |
|-----------|----------|-------------|-----------|
| Average weekly hours | <42 | 42-50 | >50 |
| Vacation usage | >90% of allowance | 60-90% | <60% |
| After-hours email/Slack | Rare | Occasional | Constant |
| Sick days (mental health) | <3/year | 3-6/year | >6/year |
| eNPS trend | Stable/improving | Declining | Negative |
| Voluntary turnover | <10% | 10-20% | >20% |

### Step 2: Mental Health Program

**Program Components:**

| Component | Description | Cost/Employee | Impact |
|-----------|------------|--------------|--------|
| **EAP (Employee Assistance Program)** | Confidential counseling (phone/video, 6-8 sessions) | EUR 3-8/mo | Very High |
| **Mental health days** | 2-3 additional paid days for mental health (no questions) | EUR 0 (productivity cost) | High |
| **Therapy subsidy** | 50-100% coverage for psychology/psychiatry | EUR 50-200/mo | Very High |
| **Mental health first aid training** | Train managers to recognize and respond to distress | EUR 200-500/person | High |
| **Meditation / mindfulness app** | Calm, Headspace, or similar subscription | EUR 5-10/mo | Medium |
| **Stress management workshops** | Quarterly workshops on resilience, coping strategies | EUR 500-1000/session | Medium |
| **Peer support network** | Trained peer listeners (voluntary, confidential) | EUR 0 (training cost) | Medium |

**PT Context:**
- SNS provides mental health services (wait times vary)
- Private health insurance often includes psychology (check coverage)
- Occupational medicine requirements (Medicina do Trabalho) include psychosocial risk assessment

### Step 3: Burnout Prevention Framework

**Prevention Levels:**

| Level | Actions | Responsibility |
|-------|---------|---------------|
| **Organizational** | Workload management, realistic goals, adequate staffing | Leadership + HR |
| **Team** | Clear expectations, cross-training, no hero culture | Managers |
| **Individual** | Boundary setting, time management, self-care | Employee (supported by org) |

**Organizational Anti-Burnout Policies:**

| Policy | Description | Implementation |
|--------|-------------|----------------|
| **No-meeting days** | 1-2 days/week with no scheduled meetings | Block calendars, enforce |
| **Meeting-free hours** | Core focus time blocks (e.g., mornings) | Team agreement |
| **Maximum meeting duration** | 25 min (not 30), 50 min (not 60) | Calendar defaults |
| **Right to disconnect** | No work communication outside hours | PT Law Art. 199-A CT |
| **Mandatory vacation** | Require minimum 10 consecutive days once/year | Policy + tracking |
| **Workload reviews** | Quarterly capacity vs demand review per person | Manager + HR |
| **Overtime tracking** | Monitor and cap excessive hours | HR dashboard |
| **Sabbatical program** | Unpaid leave option after X years (3-12 months) | Policy |

### Step 4: Flexible Work Policy

**Flexible Work Menu:**

| Option | Description | Eligibility | Approval |
|--------|-------------|-------------|----------|
| **Remote work** | Work from home X days/week | All roles where feasible | Manager |
| **Flexible hours** | Start between 7-10, end 8h later | All employees | Manager |

| **Part-time** | Reduced hours (50-80%) | Any employee (right in PT after parental leave) | HR |
| **Job sharing** | Two people share one full-time role | Pilot basis | HR + Manager |
| **Location flexibility** | Work from any location (domestic) | Remote-eligible roles | Manager |
| **Workation** | Work remotely from vacation destination (limited period) | Per policy | Manager |

### Step 8: Crisis Response Protocol

**For acute mental health situations:**

| Situation | Immediate Response | Follow-Up |
|-----------|-------------------|-----------|
| **Employee in distress** | Listen, don't diagnose, offer EAP, ensure safety | Check in next day, connect with HR |
| **Grief / loss** | Immediate time off (faltas justificadas), express support | Flexible return, adjust workload |
| **Severe burnout** | Medical leave recommendation, cover workload | Phased return plan, workload review |
| **Workplace conflict** | Separate parties, investigate, mediate | Follow-up, policy review |
| **Substance abuse** | EAP referral, no stigma, support recovery | Accommodation during treatment |

## Red Flags

Stop and warn the user if:
- Average weekly hours exceed 48 for any team (PT legal limit)
- Vacation days unused above 30% (burnout indicator, legal risk)
- Sick days trending up significantly (organizational health problem)
- No EAP or mental health support available
- Right to disconnect not respected (PT legal requirement since 2021)
- Managers unable to recognize burnout signs
- "Always on" culture celebrated (toxic productivity)
- No flexible work options offered without justification
- Wellbeing programs are cosmetic (fruit bowls) without addressing root causes
- Exit interviews cite stress/burnout as top reason
- Zero budget allocated for wellbeing programs
- Overtime consistently untracked or unreported

## 7. Status checklist per data point (Gate 7)

Every numeric value, name, or factual claim in **PESSOA Employee Wellbeing** output must carry an EXPLICIT label so the reader knows what to trust as-is and what needs verification before action.

- 🔵 **verified** — confirmed from a primary source (file, audit log, session memory, RAG hit, screen-shared confirmation)
- 🟡 **assumed** — plausible default that needs client/owner confirmation before being acted on
- 🟢 **projection** — estimate by design (timeline, cost, throughput, conversion); not verifiable today

**Why this gate exists:** outputs that mix verified facts with assumptions and projections without flagging them produce false certainty. The reader either over-trusts (acts on a guess) or under-trusts (ignores even the verified parts). Explicit labels restore signal.

**How to apply in PESSOA Employee Wellbeing:**

1. After drafting the deliverable, scan it for every concrete claim (number, name, date, metric, status, recommendation).
2. Attach one of the three labels inline; if you can't pick a label confidently, the claim isn't ready to ship.
3. Add a short citation in parentheses for 🔵 items (file path, source, dashboard) and a short condition for 🟡 / 🟢 items (what would confirm or refute it).
4. End the deliverable with a 1-line summary of how many items in each category, e.g. `Status mix: 8 🔵 · 3 🟡 · 2 🟢`.

❌ **NOT delivery-ready:**

```
Conversion rate is 18%. CAC is R$ 420. We will hit 1k MAU in Q3.
```

✅ **Delivery-ready:**

```
- Conversion rate: 18% 🔵 verified (Mixpanel funnel report 2026-05-19, n=1,242 sessions)
- CAC: R$ 420 🟡 assumed (calculated from May spend ÷ May customers; CFO has not signed off yet)
- 1k MAU in Q3 🟢 projection (linear extrapolation of last 8 weeks; assumes no churn spike)

Status mix: 1 🔵 · 1 🟡 · 1 🟢
```

**Ship checklist post-cliente-sync:**
- [ ] All 🟡 items confirmed (or downgraded to 🟢 / dropped)
- [ ] All 🔵 citations actually exist (no broken file paths, no imagined sources)
- [ ] All 🟢 projections labeled as such to the client — never presented as commitments
